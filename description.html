B203: Technology and Humanity
Simulator Description

Why Abacus?

Back in the day when I was in first or second grade, I would always see the cashiers in the old small store around the corner of my house using the abacus incredibly fast to calculate the amount of change to give me. Used to the electronic calculators, I remember looking at the cashiers so confused and having absolutely no idea how the abacus is even helpful to them at all, let alone understanding how they perform these calculations in split seconds. I am still far away from mastering this technology, so I created this project to travel back in time and “solve” the mystery of my childhood.

Why Roman Abacus?

In our Technology and Humanity in Ancient World course, we have studied Roman numeracy and looked at how certain appliances used for calculation constitute technology in ancient context. While Romans were far away from being exceptional at math, they relied on very simple tools to solve practical problems such as taxation calculation or voter counting. As I started researching abaci, I found a variety of their types and realized I was only familiar with the Russian abacus, so I wanted to challenge myself to create Roman abacus to learn a new calculating system.

I believe that using a high-level software that heavily relies on computation in order to create something so fundamental to mathematics as abacus would help me look at the ancient technology from another perspective.
